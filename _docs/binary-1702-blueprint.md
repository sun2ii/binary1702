## PART 4. SALES PROCESS & PROPOSAL SYSTEM

### 1. The Core Sales Problem

Right now, the risk is: conversations are unstructured, pricing is reactive, scope is unclear, and deals depend on improvisation.

**Required Shift:** Sales must become a defined process, not a conversation that "just happens".

### 2. Sales Process Overview — Binary 1702 LLC's 5-Step Sales System

```
Lead Capture → Qualification → Discovery Call → Solution Presentation → Proposal & Closing
```

1. **Lead Capture:** Collect prospects via multi-channel forms
2. **Qualification:** Assess fit with automated criteria
3. **Discovery Call:** Understand needs and objectives
4. **Solution Presentation:** Demonstrate tailored recommendations
5. **Proposal & Closing:** Send proposal, negotiate, and close

This structured 5-step system ensures every deal is handled with clarity, professionalism, and defined outcomes — eliminating improvisation from the sales process.

### 3. STEP 1 — LEAD CAPTURE

Leads may come from: networking, referrals, website, funnels, and outreach.

**Required Action:** Every lead must go into a system (GoHighLevel, Notion or other CRM). No exceptions.

**Minimum Data Collected:**
- Name
- Business
- Phone
- Email
- Type of request

**Rule:** If the lead is not in the system, it does not exist.

### 4. STEP 2 — QUALIFICATION

Before booking a call, the lead must be filtered.

#### Key Questions:
- What does the business do?
- What problem are they trying to solve?
- Do they already use any systems?
- What is their urgency?
- Are they ready to invest?

**Goal:** Avoid low-budget clients, unclear projects, and time-wasters.

**Rule:** Not every lead deserves a call.

### 5. STEP 3 — DISCOVERY CALL (STRUCTURED)

This is the most important step. The goal is NOT to sell immediately. The goal is to diagnose and position the solution.

1. **Opening (Control the call):** "Let me understand your current situation so I can recommend the best solution."

2. **Diagnose:**
   - How are you currently handling leads?
   - Where are you losing opportunities?
   - What systems are you using today?
   - What is not working?
   - What would an ideal solution look like?

3. **Identify Pain:** Clearly understand lost revenue, inefficiencies, manual work, and missed follow-ups.

4. **Quantify Impact:** "How many leads do you lose per week?" — This turns the conversation into value.

5. **Position the Solution:** "Based on what you shared, there are a few areas we can improve…" — Do not give full solution or price during discovery.

### 6. STEP 4 — SOLUTION PRESENTATION

This can be the same call (if simple) or a second call (recommended for higher ticket).

1. **Problem Summary:** "You mentioned that you are losing leads because there is no follow-up system…"

2. **Solution:** "What we would implement is a system that captures leads, automates follow-up, and organizes your pipeline…"

3. **Deliverables:** Explain clearly: CRM, Automations, Website / funnel, AI workflows, Software (if applicable)

4. **Outcome:** "This will allow you to respond faster, capture more opportunities, and operate more efficiently."

**Rule:** Sell the outcome, not the features.

### 7. STEP 5 — PROPOSAL & CLOSING

#### 7.1 Proposal Structure

Every proposal must include:

1. Client problem
2. Proposed solution
3. Scope of work
4. Deliverables
5. Timeline
6. Investment
7. Payment terms
8. Responsibilities
9. Next steps

#### 7.2 Proposal Delivery

Never send without context.

**Correct approach:** "I've prepared a structured proposal based on our conversation. Let me walk you through it."

#### 7.3 Closing the Deal

After presenting, ask: "Does this align with what you're looking for?"

Then: "If everything looks good, the next step is to move forward with the agreement and initial payment so we can begin."

### 8. Handling Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. Is there anything specific you'd like me to clarify or adjust before you make a decision?" |
| "It's too expensive" | "I understand. This solution is designed to solve [problem] and improve [result]. If needed, we can adjust the scope but not reduce the quality of the solution." |
| "Can you lower the price?" | "We can adjust the scope if needed, but the pricing reflects the value and structure required to implement this properly." |

### 9. Follow-Up System (CRITICAL)

Most deals are lost here.

1. **Day 1:** Send proposal
2. **Day 2:** Follow-up message
3. **Day 4:** Reminder
4. **Day 7:** Final follow-up

**Example:** "Just checking in — did you have a chance to review the proposal?"

**Rule:** Follow-up is part of the sales process, not optional.

### 10. Sales Discipline Rules

Binary 1702 LLC must operate with:

- No verbal agreements
- No starting without payment
- No undefined scope
- No emotional decisions

### 11. Positioning Rule (VERY IMPORTANT)

**Ben must stop acting like:** a developer waiting for instructions

**And start acting like:** a consultant leading the solution

### 12. Sales Identity Shift

|-----|-----|

### 13. Conversion Strategy

#### Not:

- Trying to impress

## PART 5. CONTRACTS, LEGAL STRUCTURE & PROTECTION

### 1. The Core Legal Problem

Without proper legal structure, Binary 1702 LLC is exposed to:
- Undefined scope and unlimited requests
- Clients requesting additional work without compensation
- Confusion over ownership of software and systems
- Payment delays or non-payment
- Misuse of systems or deliverables
- Future disputes and misunderstandings

**Required Shift:** Binary 1702 LLC must operate with mandatory documentation and defined agreements before any work begins.

### 2. Mandatory Documents (Non-Negotiable)

1. NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
2. Service Agreement (Main Contract)
3. Scope of Work (SOW)
4. Payment Terms
5. Ownership & Licensing Clause
6. Change Order Policy

### 3. NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)

#### Purpose — The NDA protects:
- Business ideas
- System logic
- Workflows and automations
- Client data
- Proprietary information

#### When to Use:
- Before discussing sensitive projects
- Before sharing system architecture
- Before reviewing internal processes

**Rule:** No sensitive information should be shared without a signed NDA when applicable.

### 4. Service Agreement (Main Contract)

This is the foundation of the client relationship.

#### Must Include:
- Parties involved
- Description of services
- Reference to scope of work
- Timeline
- Payment structure
- Responsibilities of each party
- Limitations of service
- Termination conditions

**Core Rule:** If it is not written in the agreement, it is not part of the project.

### 5. Scope of Work (SOW)

This is the most critical document to prevent conflict.

#### Must Clearly Define:
- What is included
- What is not included
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Dependencies
- Client responsibilities

#### Example:

**Included:** CRM setup, 1 pipeline, automation workflows

**Not included:** additional pages, advanced integrations, ongoing modifications

**Rule:** A clear scope protects time, pricing, and expectations.

### 6. Payment Terms

| Type | Terms |
|------|-------|
| Projects | 50% upfront → 50% before final delivery |
| Larger Projects | 50% upfront → 25% mid-project → 25% upon completion |
| Recurring Services | Monthly payment in advance |

**Rules:**
- No payment → no work begins
- No final payment → no final delivery

### 7. Ownership & Licensing Clause (CRITICAL)

#### Option A — Client Ownership
The client owns the final product. Binary 1702 LLC retains the right to reuse frameworks, code structures, and non-exclusive components.

#### Option B — Licensing Model
The client pays for usage rights. Binary 1702 LLC retains ownership of the system.

**Recommendation:** Default to client owns the final product; Binary retains reusable intellectual property.

**Rule:** Ownership must always be defined before development begins.

### 8. Client Responsibility Clause

#### The client is responsible for:
- Software subscriptions (GoHighLevel, etc.)
- API usage (OpenAI, Twilio, Stripe, etc.)
- Hosting (AWS, Vercel, etc.)
- Domains and third-party tools

#### Binary 1702 LLC is responsible for:
- Development
- Implementation
- System setup

**Rule:** Binary 1702 LLC does not absorb third-party operational costs.

### 9. Change Order Policy

This protects against scope creep.

Any request outside the defined scope must be:
1. Documented →
2. Quoted →
3. Approved →
4. Paid (if applicable) → before execution

**Example Response:** "That request falls outside the current scope. I can prepare an additional quote for that."

### 10-13. Additional Legal Clauses

#### Timeline & Delays
Define project timeline, dependencies on client, and approval requirements. Delays caused by the client may extend the delivery timeline.

#### Termination Clause
Define conditions for termination, payment obligations, and work already completed. Work completed must still be paid, even if the project is terminated early.

#### Liability Limitation
Binary 1702 LLC is not responsible for business outcomes, revenue results, client misuse of systems, or third-party failures. The company provides systems and implementation, not guaranteed financial results.

#### Communication & Approval
Define how approvals are given, who is the main contact, and response expectations. No major change or step is executed without approval.

### 14. Legal Discipline Rule

Binary 1702 LLC must never:
- Start projects without a signed agreement
- Work without defined scope
- Accept verbal-only agreements
- Deliver final product without final payment

### 15. Strategic Protection Principle

Contracts are not a formality. They are a business protection system.

### 16. Final Legal Positioning

Binary 1702 LLC operates as a structured company that defines expectations, documents agreements, protects its work, and maintains professional boundaries.

> "If you don't define the rules, the client will define them for you."
